## Introduction

The mass adoption of **artificial intelligence systems** in companies presents unprecedented regulatory, ethical, and operational challenges. As law firms advising corporate clients, our ability to design and implement **AI governance frameworks** has become a critical competency.

This article provides a practical guide to help your corporate clients establish robust governance structures that enable responsible AI deployment while remaining aligned with the **EU AI Act**, **GDPR**, and other applicable regulations.

## 1. What is an AI Governance Framework?

### Definition and Scope

An **AI governance framework** is a set of policies, processes, roles, and controls that guide how a company develops, deploys, and oversees AI systems in a responsible and compliant manner.

### Key Components

An effective framework includes:

| Component | Description | Elements |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Governance** | Decision-making structures | AI Committee, roles, responsibilities |
| **Risk Management** | Identification and mitigation | DPIA assessments, risk classification |
| **Compliance** | Regulatory alignment | AI Act, GDPR, sectoral regulations |
| **Transparency** | Explainability and communication | Documentation, user explanations |
| **Oversight** | Continuous monitoring | Metrics, alerts, audits |

## 2. Legal and Regulatory Foundation

### EU AI Act: Governance Obligations

The **EU AI Act 2026** establishes specific governance requirements for AI systems based on their risk level:

**High-Risk AI Systems:**
- Mandatory quality management system
- AI system registration
- Comprehensive technical documentation
- Human oversight
- Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity
- Data governance guarantees

**General-Purpose AI:**
- Systemic risk assessment
- Cybersecurity guarantees
- Correction measures
- Training transparency

### GDPR and Privacy

Relevant GDPR obligations include:
- **Impact Assessment (DPIA)** for AI processing personal data
- **Data minimization** in training and inference
- **Right to explanation** (Art. 22) for automated decisions
- **Traceability** of automated decisions

### Sectoral Regulations

Depending on the sector, applicable regulations may include:
- **Financial sector:** DORA, MiFID II, Basel III
- **Healthcare:** MDR, GDPR-health, ISO 13485 standards
- **Insurance:** Solvency II, EIOPA guidelines

## 3. Designing the AI Governance Framework

### Step 1: AI System Inventory and Classification

Create a centralized registry of all AI systems:

```markdown
# AI System Inventory Template

| ID | System | AI Type | Use Case | Risk Level | Implementation Date | Owner |
|----|---------|---------|----------|------------|-------------------|-------|
| AI-001 | Customer Chatbot | Generative | Customer Service | Low | 2025-06-15 | Marketing |
| AI-002 | Credit Scoring | Predictive | Risk Evaluation | High | 2025-08-20 | Risk |
| AI-003 | Fraud Detection | Anomaly Analysis | Compliance | High | 2025-10-01 | Compliance |
```

### Step 2: Governance Structure

#### AI Committee

**Role:** Highest decision-making body for AI

**Composition:**
- CEO/COO (chairperson)
- CLO (Chief Legal Officer)
- CISO (Chief Information Security Officer)
- CTO/CIO
- DPO (Data Protection Officer)
- AI Ethics Officer
- HR Representative

**Responsibilities:**
- Approve governance frameworks and policies
- Review and approve high-risk systems
- Oversee regulatory compliance
- Make decisions on AI incidents

**Meeting frequency:** Monthly (quarterly)

#### Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities | Profile |
|------|------------------|---------|
| **AI Governance Officer** | Lead framework implementation | Senior Legal/Compliance |
| **AI Product Owner** | Responsible for specific system | Product/Tech |
| **AI Ethics Officer** | Ensure ethical use | Philosophy/Ethics |
| **AI Risk Manager** | Manage AI risks | Risk/Compliance |
| **Human-in-the-Loop** | Decision oversight | Domain expert |

### Step 3: Policies and Procedures

#### AI Use Policy

**Minimum content:**
- Ethical principles and values
- Permitted/prohibited use cases
- Transparency requirements
- Privacy and data protection rules
- Escalation procedures

**Example principle:**
> *"All AI systems must be designed and operated in a way that respects fundamental human rights, dignity, and individual autonomy. AI must not be used for discriminatory purposes or perpetuate unjustified biases."*

#### AI Risk Assessment Procedure

**Workflow:**
1. **Initial inventory** → Identify system
2. **Risk classification** → Low/Medium/High (per AI Act)
3. **Impact analysis** → DPIA (GDPR)
4. **Bias evaluation** → Fairness assessment
5. **Security review** → Cybersecurity and robustness
6. **Approval** → AI Committee

### Step 4: Controls and Monitoring

#### Pre-Implementation Controls

- [ ] DPIA completed and approved
- [ ] Bias analysis performed
- [ ] Security evaluation (penetration test)
- [ ] Complete technical documentation
- [ ] User training completed
- [ ] Contingency plan defined

#### Operational Controls

- [ ] KPI metrics monitoring
- [ ] Decision log audits
- [ ] Periodic performance review
- [ ] User satisfaction surveys
- [ ] Incident analysis
- [ ] Periodic model retraining

#### Governance Metrics

| Metric | Target | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| AI error rate | < 5% | Monthly |
| Incident response time | < 24h | Quarterly |
| DPIA compliance | 100% | Annual |
| Staff training | 100% | Semiannual |
| External audits | 1/year | Annual |

## 5. Practical Implementation: Use Case

### Case: AI Credit Scoring System

**Context:** Fintech company deploys AI for credit risk evaluation

#### Step 1: Risk Classification
- **Type:** High-risk system (AI Act - Annex III)
- **Legal bases:** AI Act + GDPR + financial regulation

#### Step 2: Governance Structure

```
AI Committee
├── CEO (chairperson)
├── CLO (Legal)
├── CRO (Risk)
├── CISO
└── DPO

Project Team
├── AI Product Owner (Lead)
├── Data Scientists (3)
├── Compliance Analyst (1)
└── Risk Manager (1)
```

#### Step 3: Implemented Controls

**DPIA (GDPR):**
- Privacy impact assessment
- Data minimization measures
- ARCO rights response plan

**AI Act Compliance:**
- Quality management system
- Technical documentation
- Human oversight
- Accuracy and robustness validated
- Activity logging

**Fairness:**
- Gender and age disparity evaluation
- Metrics: Equal Opportunity Difference, Demographic Parity
- Training data bias mitigation

**Transparency:**
- User notification of AI usage
- Decision explanations (SHAP feature importance)
- Contestation procedure (30 days)

#### Step 4: Continuous Monitoring

**KPI Dashboard:**
- Model accuracy
- Rejection rate vs default rate
- Fairness metrics by demographic groups
- Number of contestations received
- Average incident response time

**Alerts:**
- Drift detected (distribution change)
- Bias metrics above threshold
- Error rate > 3%
- Security incidents reported

## 6. Audit and Continuous Improvement

### Annual External Audit

**Scope:**
- AI governance structure
- Risk assessment processes
- High-risk system documentation
- AI Act, GDPR, sectoral compliance
- Incidents and lessons learned

**Deliverables:**
- Audit report
- Findings and recommendations
- Corrective action plan
- Implementation dates

### Improvement Cycle

```
Plan → Do → Check → Act
  ↓      ↓      ↓      ↓
Policies → Deploy → Monitor → Improve
```

**Phases:**
1. **Plan:** Annual policy review, update per new regulations
2. **Do:** Implement improvements and new systems
3. **Check:** Monitor KPIs, execute audits
4. **Act:** Adjust framework based on findings

**The most expensive mistake in tech entrepreneurship: building something nobody wants.**

Every year, thousands of projects fail not because the technology is bad, but because **nobody validated if there was real demand** before investing time and money.

The good news: **you can validate your idea in weeks, not months**, and with very little budget.

## ðŸ§ª The 3 validation levels

### Level 1: Problem (Does the pain exist?)
**Goal:** Confirm that the problem you want to solve is real and frequent.

**How to validate:**
1. **Qualitative interviews** (10-15 people from your target audience)
   - "How do you currently solve [problem]?"
   - "How much time/money do you lose because of this?"
   - "What have you tried to solve it?"

2. **Quantitative surveys** (50-100+ responses)
   - Use Google Forms, Typeform, or Tally
   - Ask about frequency, intensity, and willingness to pay

3. **Direct observation**
   - Forums, Facebook groups, Reddit, LinkedIn
   - Do people mention this problem spontaneously?

**âœ… Green signal:** 70%+ mention the problem as "very frequent" or "frustrating."

**ðŸš© Warning sign:** You have to "convince" them it's a problem.

### Level 2: Solution (Does your proposal solve the problem?)
**Goal:** Confirm your specific approach makes sense.

**How to validate:**
1. **Landing page + email signup**
   - Explain the solution without building anything
   - "Join the waiting list" button
   - Measure conversion (visits â†’ signups)

2. **Clickable prototype** (Figma, Canva, PowerPoint)
   - Show how it would work without coding
   - Observe reactions: do they understand the proposal?

3. **Concierge MVP** (manual service before automating)
   - Do manually what technology would do later
   - Example: If you want to automate reports, first do them manually for 5 clients

**âœ… Green signal:** >15% conversion rate on landing, positive feedback in demos.

**ðŸš© Warning sign:** "Interesting" but nobody signs up or pays.

### Level 3: Viability (Would they pay for this?)
**Goal:** Confirm real willingness to pay.

**How to validate:**
1. **Pre-sale** (the most effective method)
   - Sell before building (with early adopter discount)
   - "Reserve your access for â‚¬XX, available in X weeks"
   - If they don't buy, there's no real demand

2. **Freemium with upsell**
   - Free basic version
   - Paid advanced features
   - Measure how many convert

3. **Letter of intent**
   - For B2B products
   - "If we build this, would you buy it?"
   - Ask for written commitment (non-binding, but serious)

**âœ… Green signal:** At least 10-20 people pay in advance or sign letter of intent.

**ðŸš© Warning sign:** "Let me know when it's ready," but nobody commits money.

## ðŸ’¡ Low-cost validation methods

### 1. Landing page in 1 day
**Tools:** Carrd, Webflow, Typedream
**Cost:** â‚¬0-20/month
**Includes:**
- Clear title of problem and solution
- Key benefits (3-5 points)
- Email signup
- Estimated price (optional but recommended)

### 2. Validation ads (â‚¬50-100 total)
**Platforms:** Facebook/Instagram, Google Ads
**Goal:** Measure CTR (clicks/impressions)
**Benchmark:**
- 2-5% CTR = real interest
- Less than 1% CTR = message/problem doesn't resonate

### 3. Concierge MVP (0 technology)
**Real example:**
> Before building an automated coaching platform, an entrepreneur manually coordinated 20 sessions via Calendly + Zoom. Validated demand without writing a line of code.

### 4. "Wizard of Oz" MVP
You fake automation, but it's manual behind:
- Dashboard that "generates reports" â†’ you do them in Excel
- "AI that recommends products" â†’ you choose manually
- **Only for validation, not to scale**

### 5. Community before product
**Example:** Telegram/Discord/WhatsApp group
- Gather your target audience
- Share valuable content
- Observe what they ask repeatedly
- **When you have 50-100 active members, you have early adopters ready**

## ðŸš© Common validation mistakes

### âŒ Mistake 1: Asking family and friends
**Why it fails:** They'll say yes out of affection, not real demand.
**Alternative:** Ask strangers from the target audience.

### âŒ Mistake 2: Confusing "I like it" with "I'll buy it"
**Why it fails:** Saying "good idea" is free. Paying is the real test.
**Alternative:** Ask for commitment (pre-sale, letter of intent).

### âŒ Mistake 3: Building too fast
**Why it fails:** You invest before confirming demand.
**Alternative:** Validate with minimal resources (landing + manual).

### âŒ Mistake 4: Ignoring competition
**Why it fails:** If there's no competition, there may be no market.
**Alternative:** Competition = market validation. Differentiate, don't reinvent the wheel.

### âŒ Mistake 5: Not defining success metrics beforehand
**Why it fails:** Without clear criteria, you'll always see "positive signals."
**Alternative:** Define before: "If I get X pre-sales, I continue. If not, I pivot."

## ðŸŽ¯ Complete validation checklist

- [ ] 10-20+ people bought/reserved in advance
- [ ] Estimated CAC < 30% of customer value
- [ ] Sufficient margin to be profitable

**Execution capacity:**
- [ ] I can build an MVP in 2-3 months
- [ ] I have access to early adopters for feedback
- [ ] Sufficient budget for 6 months post-launch

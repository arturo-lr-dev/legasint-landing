## Introduction

The **eIDAS 2.0 Regulation** and the **European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDI Wallet)** are transforming how citizens, businesses, and professionals interact digitally in Europe. For **Spanish law firms**, this represents both an adaptation challenge and a competitive differentiation opportunity.

In July 2026, the EUDI Wallet implementation is underway across the European Union. Spain, as one of the pioneering member states, has already begun deploying the first operational versions of the **Digital Identity Wallet**.

## What is eIDAS 2.0?

### Evolution from eIDAS 1.0

The original eIDAS Regulation (2014) established the foundations for electronic identification and trust services in Europe. However, it had significant limitations:

- **Limited interoperability** between member states
- **No unified digital wallet** for citizens
- **Low adoption** of cross-border electronic identities

### What's New in eIDAS 2.0

| Aspect | eIDAS 1.0 | eIDAS 2.0 |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Digital wallet | Did not exist | **Mandatory EUDI Wallet** |
| Verifiable attributes | Limited | **Rich and customizable** |
| Private sector | Restricted access | **Facilitated access** |
| Usability | Fragmented | **Unified experience** |

## The European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDI Wallet)

### Key Features

The EUDI Wallet is a **secure mobile application** that allows citizens to:

1. **Store digital identity** verified by member states
2. **Share selective attributes** (only what's necessary)
3. **Sign documents** with qualified electronic signature
4. **Access public and private services** cross-border

### Available Identity Attributes

```markdown
- Basic identity (name, ID, date of birth)
- Verified legal address
- Academic and professional titles
- Licenses and permits (driving, professional practice)
- Legal capacity and representation
```

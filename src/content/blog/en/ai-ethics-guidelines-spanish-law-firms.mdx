## Introduction

**Artificial intelligence** is transforming legal practice in Spain and across the European Union. While the **EU AI Act** establishes the mandatory regulatory framework, law firms need a **robust ethical framework** to guide the responsible use of these technologies.

2026 marks a turning point: the full implementation of the AI Act and the massive adoption of AI tools in the legal sector. This guide provides a practical ethical framework for Spanish law firms that wish to lead in **responsible AI adoption**.

## 1. Fundamental Ethical Principles

### 1.1 Full Transparency

Clients have the right to know when AI is used in their matters:

```
✅ Good practices:
- Inform clients about AI use in their matters
- Document the AI tools used
- Maintain records of algorithmic decisions

❌ To avoid:
- Hidden AI use without informed consent
- Presenting AI results as exclusively human work
- Concealing the limitations of tools used
```

### 1.2 Human Responsibility

**Human supervision** is a non-negotiable principle:

- **Final review**: All AI work must be reviewed by qualified lawyers
- **Assume responsibility**: Lawyers are responsible for all results, regardless of source
- **Right to appeal**: Clients must be able to question algorithmic decisions

### 1.3 Justice and Equity

AI tools cannot amplify existing biases:

- **Bias auditing**: Regularly evaluate tools to detect gender, race, or socioeconomic biases
- **Data diversity**: Ensure training sets represent Spanish society's diversity
- **Equitable access**: Don't use AI to discriminate in legal services

## 3. Ethical Categorization of AI Use

### 3.1 Acceptable Use (Low Risk)

These applications are considered **ethically acceptable**:

| Application | Ethical Risk | Measures |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| Automated legal research | Low | Primary source verification |
| Draft generation | Low | Exhaustive human review |
| Document translation | Low | Review by bilingual lawyer |
| Evidence organization | Medium | Context validation |

### 3.2 Use Requires Supervision (Medium Risk)

**Requires active human supervision**:

```typescript
// Example: contract analysis with AI
function contractAIAnalysis(contract) {
  const aiResult = analyzeWithAI(contract);

  // ✅ HUMAN SUPERVISION MANDATORY
  const humanReview = reviewByLawyer(aiResult);
  
  if (humanReview.risks.length > 0) {
    return "Requires additional review";
  }

  return "Approved with reservations";
}
```

Medium risk applications:
- **Predictive analysis** of judicial outcomes
- **Automatic classification** of legal documents
- **Information extraction** from complex contracts
- **Virtual assistants** for client service

### 3.3 Restricted Use (High Risk)

**Prohibited or highly regulated** under the AI Act:

- ⚠️ **Automated judicial decisions** (Art. 5 AI Act)
- ⚠️ **Credibility assessment** of persons
- ⚠️ **Biometric profiling** for legal identification
- ⚠️ **Emotional analysis** in negotiations

> **Note**: AI Act Article 5 prohibits certain AI practices considered unacceptable, including judicial decision-making without human supervision.

## 4. Ethical Evaluation of AI Tools

### 4.1 Evaluation Checklist

```markdown
Before adopting an AI tool, verify:

✅ Provider transparency
   - Do they document how the model works?
   - Do they provide information about training data?

✅ Accountability
   - Does the provider accept responsibility for errors?
   - Does the DPA include indemnification clauses?

✅ Regulatory compliance
   - AI Act certification (when applicable)?
   - GDPR compliance in data protection?

✅ Ethics and human rights
   - Provider's human rights policy?
   - Available appeal mechanisms?

✅ Sustainability
   - Model environmental impact?
   - Sustainable resource use policy?
```

### Case 2: AI in Legal Advisory

**Situation**: A lawyer uses ChatGPT to generate legal opinion drafts.

**Ethical principles**:
1. **Don't present as exclusively own work**: Attribute AI use
2. **Verify case law**: Tools can hallucinate cases
3. **Add human value**: Lawyer must provide analysis, not just formatting

```
Recommended process:

Step 1: Consult primary sources (BOE, case law)
Step 2: Use AI for draft structure
Step 3: Verify each statement with sources
Step 4: Add personal analysis and experience
Step 5: Finalize with rigorous legal review
```

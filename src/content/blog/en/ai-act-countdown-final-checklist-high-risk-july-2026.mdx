## Introduction

**August 2026** is just around the corner. The European Artificial Intelligence Regulation (AI Act) establishes that from that date, all **high-risk AI systems** must comply with a series of strict obligations. There are **less than 30 days** left for companies, law firms, and technology providers to ensure their systems meet regulatory requirements.

This article is a **final and urgent checklist** to identify what's missing, prioritize critical actions, and avoid penalties that can reach **7% of global annual turnover**.

---

## 1. Is Your System High-Risk? Quick Verification

Before taking action, confirm whether your system falls into the **high-risk** category under Annex III of the AI Act:

### Typically High-Risk Systems:
- **Biometrics** (facial recognition, emotion recognition)
- **Education** (evaluation systems, admissions)
- **Employment** (candidate selection, employee assessment)
- **Access to essential services** (credit, insurance, public benefits)
- **Justice** (judicial assistance, evidence analysis)
- **Migration and border control**
- **Law enforcement and emergency services**

### Key Question:
> Does your system make decisions or assist in decisions that significantly affect the rights, safety, or opportunities of natural persons?

If the answer is yes, **apply this checklist immediately**.

## 2. Urgent Checklist: 30 Days to Compliance

### ✅ Risk Management System (Art. 9)

- [ ] **Identify known and foreseeable risks** of the AI system
- [ ] **Evaluate residual risks** after mitigation measures
- [ ] **Document the risk management process** (updatable)
- [ ] **Update the assessment** after significant system changes

**Priority:** HIGH. Without this, the system cannot be legally deployed.

### ✅ Training Data (Art. 10)

- [ ] **Review training data collection practices**
- [ ] **Verify lawfulness of processing** (valid GDPR legal basis)
- [ ] **Evaluate potential biases** in datasets
- [ ] **Document limitations** and bias mitigation measures
- [ ] **Ensure data quality** (relevance, representativeness, exhaustiveness)

**Priority:** HIGH. Data protection authorities are already auditing AI data practices.

### ✅ Technical Documentation (Art. 11)

- [ ] **Create complete technical documentation** per Annex IV
- [ ] **Include system description**, architecture, parameters
- [ ] **Document training, validation, and testing processes**
- [ ] **Keep updated** after each version

**Priority:** HIGH. Mandatory to demonstrate compliance to authorities.

### ✅ Record-Keeping (Art. 12)

- [ ] **Register the system** in the EU high-risk AI database
- [ ] **Include information**: provider identity, system function, conformity status
- [ ] **Update the registry** upon significant changes

**Priority:** MEDIUM. The registry must exist before deployment.

### ✅ Transparency and User Information (Art. 13)

- [ ] **Inform users** that they are interacting with an AI system
- [ ] **Describe system capabilities and limitations**
- [ ] **Indicate the specific purpose** for which it is designed
- [ ] **Include contact details** of the responsible party (provider or deployer)

**Priority:** HIGH. Transparency is a fundamental pillar of the AI Act.

### ✅ Human Oversight (Art. 14)

- [ ] **Design interface for effective human oversight**
- [ ] **Train human operators** on system limitations
- [ ] **Establish procedures** for manual intervention
- [ ] **Ensure operators can override** automated decisions

**Priority:** HIGH. Human oversight is mandatory for all high-risk systems.

### ✅ Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity (Art. 15)

- [ ] **Validate system accuracy** with appropriate metrics
- [ ] **Test robustness** against unexpected or adversarial inputs
- [ ] **Implement cybersecurity measures** (NIS2, DORA if applicable)
- [ ] **Document testing results** and improvement plans

**Priority:** HIGH. Technical robustness is critical to prevent harm.

### ✅ Quality Management and Change Control (Art. 17)

- [ ] **Implement documented quality management system**
- [ ] **Establish change control procedures**
- [ ] **Define criteria** for "significant changes" requiring re-evaluation
- [ ] **Maintain version and modification records**

**Priority:** MEDIUM. Essential for maintaining continuous compliance.

### ✅ CE Declaration of Conformity (Art. 48)

- [ ] **Prepare CE declaration of conformity** (Annex V)
- [ ] **Include system identification**, applicable regulation, standards
- [ ] **Sign and maintain** the updated declaration

**Priority:** HIGH. Without CE marking, the system cannot be marketed in the EU.

### ✅ CE Marking (Art. 49)

- [ ] **Apply visible CE marking** on the system or documentation
- [ ] **Ensure legibility** and integrity of the marking
- [ ] **Associate CE with the corresponding** conformity declaration

**Priority:** MEDIUM. Required for legal marketing.

## 3. Role-Specific Actions

### If You Are an **AI Provider** of High-Risk Systems:

1. **Complete technical documentation** (Annex IV) — critical deadline
2. **Perform conformity assessment** (self-assessment or with notified body)
3. **Register the system** in the EU database
4. **Prepare CE declaration** and apply marking
5. **Establish post-market surveillance system**

### If You Are a **Deployer** (Professional User):

1. **Verify that the provider** meets the above obligations
2. **Implement adequate human oversight**
3. **Inform end users** that they are interacting with AI
4. **Maintain operation and automated decision records**
5. **Notify serious incidents** to the national authority

### If You Are a **Law Firm** Advising Clients:

1. **Audit client systems** against this checklist
2. **Identify compliance gaps** and prioritize by risk
3. **Advise on documentation** and procedures
4. **Assist with authority notification** if necessary
5. **Prepare defense** against potential penalties

## 4. Penalties and Non-Compliance Risks

The AI Act establishes a progressive penalty regime:

| Type of Non-Compliance | Maximum Penalty |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| Prohibited systems (Annex I) | €35M or 7% global turnover |
| High-risk obligations | €15M or 3% global turnover |
| General provisions | €7.5M or 1.5% global turnover |
| Incorrect information to authorities | €7.5M or 1% global turnover |

**Note:** Penalties are proportional to turnover, meaning **major tech companies face massive fines**.

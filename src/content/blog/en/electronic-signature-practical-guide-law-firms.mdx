## Introduction

**Electronic signature** is one of the most transformative technologies for law firms in 2026. It enables faster contract execution, reduces operational costs, and provides a modern experience for clients. However, proper implementation requires understanding applicable regulations and choosing the right tools.

In this practical guide, we analyze the **types of electronic signature**, their **legal validity** under the **eIDAS regulation**, and the **best tools** for Spanish and European law firms.

## 1. Legal Framework: eIDAS Regulation

### What is eIDAS?

**Regulation (EU) No 910/2014** (eIDAS - electronic IDentification, Authentication and trust Services) establishes the European legal framework for electronic signatures and other trust services.

**Key points:**
- ✅ Legal validity throughout the EU
- ✅ No discrimination against electronic signatures
- ✅ Technical and security requirements
- ✅ Qualified trust service providers

### Principle of Non-Discrimination (Art. 25)

> "An electronic signature shall not be denied legal effect and admissibility as evidence in legal proceedings solely on the grounds that it is in an electronic form."

**Practical implication:** Even a simple signature is valid, although it can be challenged. To avoid disputes, advanced or qualified signatures are recommended.

## 2. Types of Electronic Signature

### 2.1. Simple Electronic Signature

**Definition:** Any electronic data attached to or associated with other electronic data that serves as a method of authentication.

**Examples:**
- Checking an "I accept" box
- Typing your name in a field
- Clicking a "Sign" button
- Signature with mouse/trackpad

**Legal validity:**
- ✅ Valid under eIDAS (Art. 25)
- ⚠️ Weak against challenges
- ⚠️ No presumption of integrity

**Use cases:**
- Acceptance of internal terms
- Low-risk documents
- Informal communications

### 2.2. Advanced Electronic Signature

**Requirements (Art. 26 eIDAS):**
1. **Uniquely linked** to the signatory
2. Capable of **identifying** the signatory
3. Created using data under the signatory's **sole control**
4. Linked to the data signed in such a way that any subsequent **change** is detectable

**Technical characteristics:**
- Non-qualified digital certificate
- Multi-factor authentication (2FA)
- Cryptographic integrity (hash + encryption)

**Legal validity:**
- ✅ Greater probative value than simple signature
- ✅ Presumption of integrity (if not challenged)
- ⚠️ May require expert evidence if challenged

**Use cases:**
- Standard commercial contracts
- Relevant internal documents
- Communications between lawyers and clients

**Tools:**
- DocuSign (with SMS verification)
- Adobe Sign
- PandaDoc

### 2.3. Qualified Electronic Signature

**Definition (Art. 3.12 eIDAS):**
Advanced electronic signature created by a **qualified signature creation device** and based on a **qualified certificate**.

**Additional requirements:**
- Certificate issued by **Qualified Trust Service Provider** (QTSP)
- Secure device (cryptographic card, HSM, etc.)
- Compliance with ETSI technical standards

**Legal validity:**
- ✅ **Legal equivalence** with handwritten signature (Art. 25.2 eIDAS)
- ✅ Presumption of integrity and authorship
- ✅ Maximum probative value

**Use cases:**
- High-value contracts
- Powers of attorney
- Documents with Public Administrations
- M&A operations
- Documents requiring registry registration

**Qualified providers in Spain:**
- FNMT-RCM (National Mint)
- Camerfirma
- ANF AC
- Uanataca

## 3. Electronic Signature in Legal Practice

### 3.1. Commercial Contracts

**Recommendation:** **Advanced** electronic signature at minimum.

**Typical workflow:**
1. **Draft** the contract (Word/Google Docs)
2. **Send** via signature platform (DocuSign, Adobe Sign)
3. **Notify** parties (email with secure link)
4. **Sign** with 2FA authentication (SMS, email, etc.)
5. **Store** signed contract securely (with cryptographic hash)

**Elements to include in the contract:**
```markdown
The parties agree that this contract is signed electronically 
in accordance with eIDAS Regulation (EU) 910/2014, with advanced 
electronic signature, having full legal validity.
```

### 3.2. Powers of Attorney and Notarial Documents

**Recommendation:** **Qualified** electronic signature.

**Context:** In Spain, many notaries accept powers of attorney with qualified electronic signature, but **not all**. Always consult beforehand.

**Specialized platforms:**
- **Signaturit** (integration with FNMT)
- **Uanataca** (solutions for notaries)
- **Camerfirma** (qualified certificates)

### 3.3. Documents with Public Administration

**Requirement:** **Qualified** electronic signature or **recognized certificate**.

**Examples:**
- Electronic filing in courts (LexNET)
- Procedures with Tax Agency (AEAT)
- Electronic Commercial Registry
- Mandatory electronic notifications

**Accepted certificates:**
- DNIe (Electronic ID card)
- FNMT certificates
- Cl@ve (identification system)

### 3.4. Due Diligence and M&A

**Recommendation:** **Qualified** electronic signature + secure **data room**.

**Special considerations:**
- **NDA** (Non-Disclosure Agreement) signed before accessing documents
- Detailed **audit logs** (who accessed, when, what was signed)
- **End-to-end encryption** of sensitive documents
- **Limited retention** post-closing (GDPR compliance)

## 6. Security and GDPR Aspects

### 6.1. Data Protection

**GDPR consideration:** Signature platforms process personal data (name, email, ID, signature, etc.).

**Mandatory requirements:**
- ✅ **DPA** (Data Processing Agreement) with provider
- ✅ Server in **EU** (or standard contractual clauses)
- ✅ **Encryption** in transit and at rest
- ✅ **Audit logs** (who accessed, when)
- ✅ **Right to erasure** post-operation

**Recommended European providers:**
- Signaturit (Spain)
- Uanataca (Spain)
- DocuSign (EU servers available)

### 6.2. Secure Storage

**Best practices:**
- Store signed contracts in **encrypted** system
- Maintain **immutable logs** (blockchain optional)
- **Redundant backup** (3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 media, 1 offsite)
- **Limited retention** according to contract type

**Typical retention period:**
- Commercial contracts: 5-10 years
- Powers of attorney: While valid + 5 years
- Employment documents: 4 years (statute of limitations)

### 6.3. Strong Authentication

**Recommendation:** Enable **2FA** (Two-Factor Authentication) for all users.

**2FA methods:**
- SMS (less secure, but widely accepted)
- Email with code
- Authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy)
- Biometrics (TouchID, FaceID)

## 8. Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

### Mistake 1: Using Simple Signature for Critical Contracts

**Problem:** Client signs with "click" without authentication → challenges contract.

**Solution:** Use **advanced** signature minimum, with 2FA (SMS/email).

### Mistake 2: Not Including Electronic Signature Clause

**Problem:** Contract doesn't mention it's signed electronically → client questions validity.

**Solution:** Always include:
```
The parties expressly agree to the electronic signature of this contract 
in accordance with eIDAS Regulation (EU) 910/2014, having full legal validity.
```

### Mistake 3: Provider Without GDPR DPA

**Problem:** Provider doesn't sign DPA → GDPR non-compliance → possible sanction.

**Solution:** Require DPA before contracting. Check on their website or ask sales.

### Mistake 4: Not Preserving Audit Logs

**Problem:** Signature is challenged and you have no proof of who/when/how signed.

**Solution:** Download and archive **audit certificate** along with signed contract.

### Mistake 5: Relying on Screenshots

**Problem:** Using screenshots as "proof" of signature → easily falsifiable.

**Solution:** Use provider's **cryptographic certificate** (SHA-256 hash, timestamp, etc.).

## Introduction

Digital transformation is not an IT project: it's a business model change. And like any structural change, it needs a clear plan, defined phases, and metrics that tell you if you're on the right track.

In this article, we present a **practical 90-day roadmap** for digitalizing a law firm. This isn't theory: these are the phases we've implemented with dozens of firms, from 5-lawyer boutiques to international practices.

The goal isn't to use more tools. It's to work better, bill more, and sleep soundly.

---

## Phase 1: Diagnosis and Prioritization (Days 1-14)

### Quick Digital Audit

Before buying software, understand where you stand. Evaluate these 5 pillars:

| Pillar | Key Question | Risk if Failing |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Document Management** | Where do you store case files? | Information loss, GDPR non-compliance |
| **Communication** | How do you communicate with clients? | Poor experience, client churn |
| **Processes** | What tasks repeat weekly? | Lost hours, human error |
| **Billing** | How long does it take to issue an invoice? | Deteriorated cash flow |
| **Security** | Do you have 2FA, backup, encryption? | GDPR fines, reputational damage |

### Team Interviews

Don't digitalize over your team's head. Talk to:
- **Partners**: What keeps them up at night?
- **Lawyers**: What tasks do they hate?
- **Admin**: Where do processes get stuck?

### Identified Quick Wins

By the end of this phase, you should have:
1. **Pain map**: the 3 biggest sources of inefficiency
2. **Approximate budget**: investment needed per phase
3. **Quick wins**: 2-3 changes you can make this week

> 💡 **Tip**: Don't try to digitalize everything at once. 80% of the benefit comes from 20% of processes.

## Phase 2: Core Infrastructure (Days 15-35)

### The Minimum Viable Stack

You don't need 20 tools. You need 5 well-chosen ones:

```
📁 Document Management: SharePoint / Google Workspace / Nextcloud
💬 Communication: Teams / Slack (with client channels)
📋 Task Management: Notion / ClickUp / Trello
💰 Billing: Holded / Quipu / Legal-specific software
🔐 Security: Mandatory 2FA + automatic backup + VPN
```

### Data Migration

**Golden rule**: don't migrate everything. Migrate:
- Active cases (last 2 years)
- Contract templates
- Client database

For the rest, archive digitally with OCR and metadata search.

### Security Configuration

This is the most boring phase and the most important:

- [ ] **2FA** on all accounts (no exceptions)
- [ ] **Automatic backup** daily + monthly in separate location
- [ ] **Encryption** of disks and communications
- [ ] **Password policy** with manager (1Password, Bitwarden)
- [ ] **VPN** for remote access
- [ ] **Data inventory**: what you have, where it is, who accesses it

## Phase 3: Core Process Automation (Days 36-60)

### The 3 Processes That Steal the Most Time

According to our data, these are the most time-consuming processes in traditional firms:

#### 1. Client Onboarding

**Before**: Email → PDF Form → Signature → Scan → File
**After**: Online form → Electronic signature → Auto CRM → Auto folder

**Tools**: Typeform/JotForm + Signaturit/DocuSign + Zapier/n8n

#### 2. Deadline Tracking

**Before**: Excel + calendar + memory
**After**: Management system with automatic alerts

**Tools**: Clio/MyCase/Lexnet (depending on jurisdiction) or shared calendar with automations

#### 3. Document Generation

**Before**: Copy and paste from Word, find and replace
**After**: Templates with dynamic fields

**Tools**: Documate / Woodpecker / Automation with Google Docs + Apps Script

### Metrics for This Phase

| Metric | Before | 60-Day Goal |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Client onboarding time | 2-3 days | < 2 hours |
| Standard contract generation | 4 hours | < 30 minutes |
| Missed deadlines per year | > 5 | 0 |
| Invoices issued > 30 days | > 30% | < 10% |

## Phase 4: Client Experience (Days 61-80)

### Client Portal

Clients in 2026 expect the same as from their bank: online access, transparency, and self-service.

**Minimum viable**:
- Real-time case status
- Shared documents (secure)
- Centralized communication (not 50 emails)
- Online invoices and payments

**Tools**: Your practice management portal, or extranet with Notion/SharePoint

### Proactive Communication

Don't wait for the client to ask. Automate:
- Document receipt confirmation
- Weekly status updates
- Upcoming deadline alerts
- Friendly payment reminders

### Feedback Loop

Implement automatic NPS survey at case closure. The most valuable data isn't in billable hours: it's in client satisfaction.

## Phase 5: Optimization and Scaling (Days 81-90)

### Metrics Review

By day 90, you should be clear on:

```markdown
📊 Time saved per week (goal: > 10h/lawyer)
📊 Clients served with same team (goal: +30%)
📊 Client satisfaction (NPS goal: > 50)
📊 Average collection time (goal: < 30 days)
📊 Operational errors (goal: -50%)
```

### Process Documentation

What isn't documented doesn't scale. Create playbooks for:
- New lawyer onboarding
- Client intake procedure
- Incident response protocol
- Tool usage policy

### Continuous Training Plan

Technology changes. Your team must change with it:
- 30-minute monthly sessions (not 4-hour marathons)
- "Digital champion" per practice area
- Budget for experimentation (try new tools)

## Common Mistakes That Accelerate Failure

### 1. Buying Tools Before Understanding the Problem

Software doesn't fix broken processes. Optimize first, then automate.

### 2. Ignoring Change Resistance

70% of digital transformations fail because of people, not technology. Involve them from day 1.

### 3. Wanting Perfection in Phase 1

Better an 80% working system in 30 days than a perfect one that never arrives. Iterate.

### 4. Forgetting Security

A digitalized firm without security is a firm with the door wide open. Non-negotiable: 2FA, backup, encryption.

### 5. Not Measuring

If you don't measure, you don't know if it works. Define KPIs on day 1 and review weekly.

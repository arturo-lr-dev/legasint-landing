## Introduction

In the world of mergers and acquisitions, time is money. A traditional due diligence process can take weeks or months: thousands of contracts, financial statements, regulatory records, and employment documents to review manually. Today, **artificial intelligence** is transforming this process, compressing timelines to days and allowing legal teams to focus on what truly matters: strategic analysis.

In this article, we explore how to apply AI tools in M&A transactions, what platforms are available, and how to do it safely and in compliance with current regulations.

## What is AI-Assisted Due Diligence?

AI-assisted due diligence combines **natural language processing (NLP)**, **optical character recognition (OCR)**, and **language models** to:

- Automatically classify and tag documents
- Extract relevant clauses (penalties, change of control, exclusivity)
- Detect contractual anomalies and risks
- Generate executive summaries by category
- Answer questions about the document repository

The result is a review up to **10 times faster** with a lower margin for human error.

## Leading Market Tools in 2026

### 1. Luminance

**Specialization:** Legal due diligence with proprietary AI

Luminance is one of the most mature platforms for document review in M&A. It uses its own AI model trained on legal contracts to:
- Detect non-standard clauses
- Compare them against market benchmarks
- Automatically classify documents into legal categories

**Advantages:** High accuracy on legal documents in English and Spanish. GDPR-compliant.

**Ideal for:** Large transactions with high document volume (+5,000 documents).

### 2. Kira Systems (now part of Litera)

**Specialization:** Clause and provision extraction

Kira excels at identifying over 1,000 different types of clauses and allows custom model training for the specific needs of each transaction.

**Advantages:** Integration with Word and document management systems. High customization.

**Ideal for:** Teams already using Litera or needing workflows integrated with Office.

### 3. Relativity + RelativityOne

**Specialization:** E-discovery and complex document analysis

Widely used in litigation, but increasingly present in M&A for transactions with regulatory components or where post-closing litigation is expected.

**Advantages:** Powerful for forensic analysis and evidence correlation.

**Ideal for:** Transactions with regulatory exposure (antitrust, sanctions).

### 4. Harvey AI

**Specialization:** AI assistant for lawyers (GPT-4 based)

Harvey allows lawyers to ask natural language questions about document repositories: "Are there any contracts with perpetual exclusivity clauses?" or "Show me all agreements expiring in 2026."

**Advantages:** Intuitive conversational interface. Continuous model updates.

**Ideal for:** Teams that want to democratize AI access without a technical learning curve.

### 5. Custom Solutions with Claude / GPT-4

For transactions where confidentiality is critical, some law firms and investment banks opt to deploy models on their own infrastructure (on-premise or private VPC).

**Advantages:** Full control over data. No risk of model training with your documents.

**Consideration:** Requires a technical team or specialized partner.

## AI Workflow in an M&A Due Diligence

### Phase 1: Ingestion and Classification (Day 1-2)

```markdown
1. Document upload to VDR (Virtual Data Room)
2. Automatic OCR on scanned PDFs
3. AI classification by category:
   - Commercial contracts
   - Employment documentation
   - Intellectual property
   - Regulatory records
   - Pending litigation
   - Financial documentation
4. Prioritization by detected risk level
```

### Phase 2: Extraction and Analysis (Day 2-7)

The AI automatically extracts:

| Clause Type | M&A Impact |
|-------------|-----------|
| Change of control | Can trigger termination or renegotiation |
| Non-compete | Affects key management post-closing |
| IP assignment | Defines ownership of key assets |
| Material adverse change | Can block closing |
| Indemnification caps | Determines buyer's exposure |

### Phase 3: Human Review and Escalation (Day 5-12)

Lawyers review the highest-risk findings:
- Clauses flagged as non-standard
- Contracts with potentially problematic conditions
- Incomplete or missing documents

**Golden rule:** AI prioritizes, the lawyer decides.

### Phase 4: Reporting and Negotiation (Day 10-15)

The AI generates:
- Executive summaries by area
- Automated risk matrices
- Clause comparisons across similar contracts
- Issues lists for warranty negotiation

## Legal and Compliance Considerations

### GDPR and Confidentiality

When using cloud platforms for due diligence, always verify:

- ✅ **Signed DPA** with the AI provider
- ✅ **Storage in the EU** (or approved adequacy decision)
- ✅ **No model training** with your documents
- ✅ **Audit logs** of VDR access
- ✅ **Guaranteed deletion** after transaction closing

### AI Act (Applicable from 2026)

AI tools used in due diligence may be classified as **limited-risk** systems under the AI Act, which implies:

- Obligation to inform users they are interacting with AI
- Basic technical documentation of the system
- No independent conformity assessment required

However, if AI makes decisions significantly affecting individuals (e.g., labor analysis with employment impact), it could be elevated to **high-risk**, with additional requirements.

### Attorney-Client Privilege

**Critical point:** Documents uploaded to external platforms may be subject to debate regarding privilege maintenance. Recommendations:

1. Use platforms with end-to-end encryption
2. Include confidentiality clauses in the provider contract
3. Limit access to the most sensitive documents
4. Document the security protocol

## Real-World Cases in Spain and Europe

**Situation:** French company acquires a Spanish startup with 3,000+ contracts (SaaS clients, partners, employees).

**AI Solution:**
- Automatic classification in 4 days (vs. 3 weeks manually)
- 47 "right of first refusal" clauses detected with third parties

**Situation:** Internal due diligence between two mid-size law firms in Madrid.

**AI Solution:**
- Client portfolio analysis (conflict of interest screening)
- Review of partner agreements and non-compete covenants

- Analysis of office lease and equipment contracts

**Result:** Merger completed without post-closing surprises — unusual for transactions in this sector.

## When to Use AI and When Not to

### ✅ Use AI when:

- Document volume exceeds 500 documents
- Transaction has a tight timeline (< 4 weeks)
- Repetitive due diligence of the same type (many similar contracts)
- You need clause benchmarking against market standards

### ⚠️ Supplement with human review when:

- Highly specialized documents (sector regulation, patents)
- Negotiations where business context is key
- Partial or ambiguous documents requiring interpretation

### ❌ Do not delegate exclusively to AI:

- Final go/no-go decisions
- Assessment of material legal risks
- Communications with the counterparty
- Professional responsibility to the client

## The Future: Agentic AI in M&A

The next frontier is **AI agents** capable of:
- Automatically requesting missing documentation from the counterparty's VDR
- Updating the issues list in real time as new documents arrive
- Suggesting warranty modifications based on findings
- Drafting sections of representations & warranties

This doesn't eliminate the lawyer; it transforms them into an **orchestra conductor** where AI handles the heavy lifting and the professional adds strategic judgment.

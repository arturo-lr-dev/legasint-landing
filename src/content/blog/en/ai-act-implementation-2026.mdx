## Introduction

March 2026 marks a fundamental milestone in artificial intelligence regulation in Europe. The **Artificial Intelligence Act (AI Act)**, formally adopted in 2024, enters its critical implementation phase, directly affecting law firms, legal departments, and legal professionals throughout the European Union.

For legal professionals in Spain, understanding the obligations arising from the AI Act is not just a matter of regulatory compliance, but a strategic opportunity to position themselves as expert advisors in one of the fastest-growing areas of digital law.

## What is the AI Act and Why Does it Matter Now?

The AI Act is the world's first comprehensive regulatory framework for artificial intelligence systems. Unlike previous regulations that treated AI tangentially, this regulation establishes:

- **Risk-based classification:** Unacceptable, high, limited, and minimal risk
- **Differentiated obligations** according to AI system type
- **Significant penalties:** Up to 7% of annual global turnover
- **Direct application** in all Member States (no national transposition required)

In 2026, implementation deadlines for high-risk systems and prohibitions on unacceptable AI practices are already in force, which means **the window for adaptation is closing**.

## High-Risk Systems in the Legal Domain

Annex III of the AI Act identifies AI systems considered **high-risk**, including those used in:

- **Administration of justice and democratic processes:** Systems assisting judicial authorities in investigating or interpreting facts and law
- **Access to essential services:** Credit scoring affecting access to legal services

### Requirements for High-Risk Systems

If a firm uses or advises on high-risk systems, it must ensure:

1. **Risk management system:** Continuous identification and mitigation
2. **Data governance:** Quality, representativeness, and absence of bias
3. **Technical documentation:** Complete system specifications
4. **Activity logging:** Automatic operation logs
5. **Transparency:** Clear information for users
6. **Human oversight:** Effective human oversight mechanisms
7. **Cybersecurity:** Protection against vulnerabilities and attacks

## Prohibited AI Practices

The AI Act expressly prohibits AI systems that:
- Manipulate human behavior using subliminal techniques
- Exploit vulnerabilities of specific groups
- Perform social scoring by public authorities
- Identify people in real-time in public spaces (except exceptions)

**Practical implication:** Firms must audit client tools to ensure they don't incorporate these prohibited functionalities.

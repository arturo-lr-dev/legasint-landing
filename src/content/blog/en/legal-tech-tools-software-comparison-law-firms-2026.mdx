## Introduction

Digital transformation in the legal sector accelerates each year. In 2026, law firms that fail to adopt the right technological tools will face a real competitive disadvantage against those who have already embraced **automation**, **artificial intelligence**, and efficient workflows.

This guide offers an up-to-date comparison of the best **legal tech** solutions available for Spanish and European law firms, organized by category with practical recommendations.

## 1. Practice Management

Integrated management platforms are the core of the digital law firm: they organize cases, tasks, schedules, and billing in one place.

### Clio

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐**

- **Best for:** Medium and large firms
- **Strengths:** Native integration with Microsoft 365, Zoom, and DocuSign; AI module for time and profitability analysis
- **Weaknesses:** High price for small firms; limited adaptation to the Spanish market
- **Approximate price:** From €49/user/month

### Lawcus

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐**

- **Best for:** Small and medium firms
- **Strengths:** Intuitive interface, workflow automation, integrated CRM
- **Weaknesses:** Fewer integrations than Clio
- **Approximate price:** From €34/user/month

### Sepín

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐**

- **Best for:** Spanish firms needing integration with national legislation
- **Strengths:** Integrated legal database, adapted to the Spanish market, local support
- **Weaknesses:** Less modern interface, lower automation capacity
- **Approximate price:** Contact provider

## 2. Document Management & AI

Document review remains one of the most time-consuming tasks in legal work. AI tools have transformed this process.

### Harvey AI

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐**

- **Best for:** Large firms with high document volumes
- **Main use:** Contract analysis, due diligence, legal research
- **2026 advantage:** Full support for European legislation post-AI Act
- **Price:** Enterprise (contact for pricing)

### Kira Systems (now part of Litera)

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐**

- **Best for:** M&A operations, mass contract review
- **Main use:** Extraction and analysis of contractual clauses
- **Advantage:** High accuracy (>90%) in clause identification
- **Price:** From €1,500/month

### Claude for Work (Anthropic)

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐**

- **Best for:** Firms needing a secure, general-purpose AI assistant
- **Main use:** Document drafting, analysis, research, summaries
- **2026 advantage:** Certified compliance with EU AI Act; on-premise option available
- **Price:** From €25/user/month (Teams plan)

> **GDPR Note:** When using any cloud AI tool, ensure you anonymize personal data before processing. See our [GDPR and AI guide for law firms](/blog/en/gdpr-ai-tools-law-firms).

## 3. Electronic Signatures

Electronic signatures are now indispensable. The question is choosing the right level based on the document type.

### DocuSign

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐**

- **Best for:** Commercial contracts, international agreements
- **Advantage:** Global recognition, universal integration
- **Signature type:** SES, AES, and QES (eIDAS)
- **Price:** From €35/user/month

### Signaturit (Spanish company)

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐**

- **Best for:** Spanish and European law firms
- **Advantage:** Spanish company, local support, eIDAS QES certification
- **Certification:** eIDAS-compliant + adapted to Spanish LSSICE
- **Price:** From €29/user/month

### Validated ID (vIDsigner)

**Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐**

- **Best for:** Notary offices and cases requiring qualified signatures
- **Advantage:** Biometric signature and QES recognized before Spanish courts
- **Price:** Contact provider

## 7. How to Choose: 5 Key Questions

Before investing in any tool, answer these questions:

**1. What is my monthly document volume?**
- Fewer than 100 documents/month → Generalist tools (Claude, basic Signaturit)
- More than 500 documents/month → Specialized platforms (Kira, Harvey)

**2. Do I work primarily with Spanish or international legislation?**
- National → Prioritize Sepín, Signaturit, vLex
- International → Westlaw, DocuSign, Harvey

**3. Do I have a DPO or privacy officer?**
- If you process personal data with AI → You need a signed DPA and GDPR review

**4. What is my realistic per-user budget?**
- Under €50/user/month → Basic but functional stacks
- Over €100/user/month → Enterprise platforms with higher ROI

**5. Do I need integration with existing tools (Office, Teams)?**
- Yes → Prioritize tools with Microsoft 365 integration

## 8. Key 2026 Trends to Follow

### Mandatory E-Invoicing

New Spanish legislation (Ley Crea y Crece) requires **electronic invoicing** between companies from 2026 onwards. Make sure your billing software is compatible with the VERI*FACTU system.

### AI Act Compliance

Since February 2026, the first obligations under the **EU AI Act** have been in force. If you use AI tools with your clients, you must:
- Verify the risk level of the tool
- Inform clients about AI use
- Maintain usage records

### Security by Design

The NIS2 directive requires firms providing services to essential entities to implement verifiable cybersecurity measures. Software selection criteria must include security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2).

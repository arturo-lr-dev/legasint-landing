## The AI Act Is Here: Is Your Law Firm Ready?

The European Union's **Artificial Intelligence Act (AI Act)** entered into force in 2024, and in 2026 we are in full implementation phase. For law firms and legal service providers using AI tools, this moment is critical: the transition period is progressing and compliance obligations are becoming increasingly demanding.

In this article, we analyze the practical requirements of the AI Act for the legal sector and provide a roadmap to ensure regulatory compliance.

## What is the AI Act and Why Does It Matter to the Legal Sector?

The AI Act is the world's first comprehensive artificial intelligence regulation. It adopts a **risk-based approach**, classifying AI systems into four categories:

- **Unacceptable risk**: Prohibited (e.g., social scoring)
- **High risk**: Strict regulation (e.g., personnel selection systems, some applications in justice)
- **Limited risk**: Transparency obligations
- **Minimal risk**: No specific regulation

For law firms, the most common tools fall into the **limited or minimal risk** categories, but it's crucial to understand the differences.

## AI Tools in Law Firms: Classification by Risk Level

### 🟢 Minimal Risk (No specific requirements)
- Spelling and grammar checkers
- Basic translation tools
- Calendar scheduling assistants
- General informational chatbots

### 🟡 Limited Risk (Transparency obligations)
- **ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini** and other LLMs for drafting
- Virtual assistants interacting with clients
- Contract analysis tools
- Legal document generation systems

**Main obligation:** Clearly inform users that they are interacting with AI.

### 🔴 High Risk (Strict regulation)
- Credit risk assessment systems
- Automated candidate selection tools
- AI used in judicial processes for evidence evaluation or sentence prediction

**Requirements:** Conformity assessment, registration in European database, exhaustive technical documentation, human oversight.

## Practical Compliance Requirements for Law Firms

### 1. AI Systems Inventory (Immediate Action)

**Create an internal registry** of all AI tools used in the firm:

```markdown
| Tool                 | Provider     | Use                  | Risk Category |
|----------------------|--------------|----------------------|---------------|
| ChatGPT Enterprise   | OpenAI       | Document drafting    | Limited       |
| Contract Analytics   | LegalTech X  | Contract review      | Limited       |
| Calendly             | Calendly     | Scheduling           | Minimal       |
```

### 2. Transparency Policies

For **limited risk** tools, implement:

- **Clear website notices:** "This firm uses AI assistants to optimize services. All communications are supervised by human lawyers."
- **Service contract clauses:** Inform about AI use in service delivery.
- **Team training:** Ensure all professionals know when and how to inform about AI use.

### 3. Human Oversight (Human-in-the-Loop)

The AI Act requires effective human oversight, especially in critical decisions:

- **Mandatory review:** No AI-generated document should be sent to clients without human review.
- **Sensitive decisions:** Procedural strategies, negotiations, complex legal interpretation → always with lawyer validation.
- **Decision logging:** Document which AI outputs were used and how they were validated.

### 4. Data Protection and Confidentiality

The AI Act complements GDPR, it doesn't replace it:

- **Impact assessments (DPIA):** Mandatory if the AI system processes personal data automatically at large scale.
- **Clear legal bases:** Consent or legitimate interest to process data with AI.
- **Anonymization:** Whenever possible, use anonymized data to train or test tools.

### 5. Contracts with AI Providers

Review and update contracts with providers (OpenAI, legal tech, etc.):

- **Compliance clauses:** Require provider compliance with the AI Act.
- **Shared responsibility:** Clarify who responds to incidents (provider vs. firm).
- **Audits:** Right to audit AI systems used.
- **Data exit:** Guarantee of portability and data deletion upon contract termination.

## AI Act Key Deadlines

| Date             | Obligation                                                      |
|------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| **February 2025**| Ban on unacceptable risk AI systems                             |
| **August 2026**  | Obligations for general purpose AI systems (GPAIs)              |
| **August 2027**  | Full requirements for high-risk systems                         |

> **Recommendation:** Don't wait for deadlines. Implementing best practices now minimizes risks and facilitates adaptation.

## AI Act Compliance Checklist for Law Firms

- [ ] **Complete inventory** of AI tools used
- [ ] **Risk classification** of each tool
- [ ] **Transparency policy** implemented (website, contracts, communications)
- [ ] **Human oversight protocol** documented and trained
- [ ] **DPIA performed** for systems processing personal data
- [ ] **Provider contracts** reviewed and updated
- [ ] **Team training** on responsible AI use and legal obligations
- [ ] **Incident log** and response procedure for AI failures

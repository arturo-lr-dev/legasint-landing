## Introduction

**Smart contracts** are revolutionizing how we think about legal agreements. But are they legally valid in Spain? Can they automate legal processes safely and in compliance with the law?

## What is a Smart Contract?

A smart contract is a **self-executing computer program** that operates on blockchain. Its main characteristics:

- **Automation:** Executes automatically when predefined conditions are met
- **Immutability:** Once deployed, it cannot be modified
- **Transparency:** The code is public and auditable
- **Decentralization:** Does not depend on intermediaries

```javascript
// Simplified smart contract example
contract Rental {
  function payRent() public {
    if (currentDate >= dueDate) {
      transfer(owner, rentAmount);
      recordPayment();
    }
  }
}
```

## Legal Framework in Spain

### Contractual Validity

According to the **Spanish Civil Code** (art. 1.278), contracts are valid regardless of their form, except for legal exceptions. Smart contracts **can have legal validity** if they meet:

1. **Consent:** Parties must understand and accept the terms
2. **Lawful object:** The contract must pursue a legal purpose
3. **Lawful cause:** The reason for the contract must be legal

### eIDAS Regulation and Electronic Signature

The **eIDAS Regulation** (EU 910/2014) recognizes the validity of electronic transactions. Smart contracts can incorporate:

- Advanced electronic signatures
- Digital identification of parties
- Verifiable timestamping

### Information Society Services Act

The **LSSI** (Law 34/2002) establishes that contracts concluded electronically have the same validity as traditional ones.

## Legal Challenges

### 1. Code Interpretation

> What prevails: the smart contract code or the documented contractual intention?

In case of conflict, Spanish courts will tend to prioritize the **literal interpretation of natural language** over code.

### 2. Liability for Errors

If a smart contract contains a **bug** that causes damages:

- Who is liable? The developer? The parties?
- Does contractual or tort liability apply?

### 3. Impossibility of Modification

Blockchain **immutability** can clash with:

- Right of withdrawal (consumers)
- Abusive clauses (which must be nullified)
- Supervening changes (*rebus sic stantibus* doctrine)

## Implementation Recommendations

### For Companies

1. **Prior legal advice:** Validate contract design with specialized lawyers
2. **Hybrid clauses:** Combine smart contract with traditional legal document
3. **Resolution mechanisms:** Include arbitration or mediation for disputes
4. **Code audit:** Verify security before deployment

### For Users

1. **Understand the code:** If you don't know programming, request technical explanation
2. **Review terms:** Smart contract must have attached explanatory document
3. **Verify identity:** Ensure parties are correctly identified
4. **Keep evidence:** Preserve all communications and contract versions

## Data Protection (GDPR)

Smart contracts must comply with **GDPR**:

- **Data minimization:** Only store strictly necessary information
- **Right to be forgotten:** Complex problem in immutable blockchain (use encryption with destructible keys)
- **Transparency:** Clearly inform about data processing

## Conclusion

Smart contracts **are legally valid in Spain** as long as they meet general contract formation requirements. However, their implementation requires:

✅ Specialized legal advice  
✅ Hybrid design (code + traditional document)  
✅ Technical and legal audit  
✅ Conflict resolution mechanisms  

The future of contractual automation is promising, but must be built on solid legal foundations.

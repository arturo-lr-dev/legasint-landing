## Introduction

In 2026, the proliferation of **audio and video deepfakes** has transformed the evidentiary landscape in courts. What was once irrefutable proof—a recording, a video call, a video statement—now demands technical scrutiny that many law firms and courts are not yet prepared to handle.

This article analyzes the current state of **digital evidence authenticity** in Spanish and European litigation, the technical standards available, and the procedural safeguards lawyers must incorporate into their strategy.

## 1. The Problem: Accessible and Sophisticated Deepfakes

### Democratization of Technology

**Voice cloning** and **video synthesis** tools are available to any user with a smartphone. In 2026, generating a fake video of someone speaking takes seconds and requires zero technical knowledge.

**Key data:**
- Over 60% of deepfakes detected in 2025-2026 are for fraud or extortion purposes
- Audio deepfakes outnumber video deepfakes by a 3:1 ratio
- Average time to detect a deepfake in a judicial context has increased from 3 days to 3 weeks

### Impact on Litigation

Deepfakes appear increasingly frequently in:
- **Civil proceedings**: proof of contractual conversations
- **Criminal**: witness statements, coercion through fake audio
- **Labor**: harassment evidence, disciplinary dismissals
- **Family law**: recordings in child custody disputes

## 2. Applicable Legal Framework

### Spain: Civil Procedure Law and Electronic Evidence

Article **326.2 LEC** already recognized the validity of electronic documents, but did not contemplate synthetic generation by AI. Recent jurisprudence (2025-2026) is developing specific criteria:

1. **Presumption of truthfulness** does not apply to multimedia files without verifiable metadata
2. **Burden of proof** falls on the party submitting the document if there are signs of manipulation
3. **Computer expert evidence** has become almost mandatory in litigation with audiovisual proof

### European Framework

The **Directive on Electronic Evidence** and **eIDAS 2.0 Regulation** (2024) provide tools for document authentication, but do not yet specifically cover AI-generated content.

**2026 updates:**
- The European Commission has proposed a **framework for certification of audiovisual content authenticity**
- The **AI Act** classifies certain synthetic generation systems as "high-risk" with marking obligations

## 3. Technical Detection and Verification Tools

### Level 1: Metadata Analysis

```bash
# EXIF metadata and digital signature extraction
exiftool recording.mp4
ffprobe -v quiet -print_format json -show_streams audio.wav
```

**What to look for:**
- Date and time consistent with the codec version used
- Digital chain of custody (SHA-256 hash from capture)
- Original device markings (serial, camera model)

### Level 2: Forensic Manipulation Analysis

Tools used by experts in 2026:

| Tool | Function | Scope |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Truepic Vision** | Pixel-level integrity analysis | Video and image |
| **Resemble Detect** | Synthetic audio detection | Voice cloning |
| **Reality Defender** | Multi-modal detection | Audio + video + text |
| **C2PA Verify** | Provenance verification | Chain of custody |

### Level 3: C2PA and Content Credentials

The **C2PA** standard (Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity) is becoming the de facto standard:

- **Content Credentials**: cryptographic metadata embedded in the file
- **Digital signatures** from camera to final file
- **Immutable record** of any editing or transformation

> ⚠️ **Limitation**: C2PA only works if the capture device supports it. Most current evidence in litigation **does not have** these credentials.

## 4. Evidentiary Strategy for Law Firms

### When Submitting Audiovisual Evidence

**Document the chain of custody from the source:**

1. Record the capture device (model, IMEI, serial)
2. Generate SHA-256 hash immediately after recording
3. Preserve the original file without compression or editing
4. Use capture applications with C2PA support (if available)
5. Request *ex ante* computer expert evidence if anticipating dispute

### When Challenging Audiovisual Evidence

**Technical and procedural arguments:**

```markdown
- [ ] Request expert evidence on file authenticity
- [ ] Demonstrate absence of C2PA or equivalent metadata
- [ ] Identify technical inconsistencies (sampling frequency, compression artifacts)
- [ ] Require the opposing party's witness declaration on method and preservation
- [ ] Alleged violation of art. 121 LEC (illicit evidence) if obtained without consent
```

### Preventive Contractual Clauses

Include in high-risk contracts:

> "The parties agree that any verbal communication relevant to the contract must be confirmed in writing or through a videoconferencing platform with authenticity recording (C2PA or equivalent). Unilateral recordings shall not constitute valid evidence unless they meet verifiable digital chain of custody standards."

## 5. Emerging Jurisprudence 2025-2026

### Spanish Supreme Court: Validity Criteria

The **STS** (recent 2025 rulings) is establishing a **"reinforced evidence"** criterion for audiovisual content:

- Direct evidence (testimony + recording) > Sole evidence (recording only)
- Recording with verifiable metadata > Compressed or WhatsApp-shared recording
- Documented chain of custody > File without technical provenance

### Court of Justice of the European Union

The CJEU has ruled on:
- **Privacy vs. evidence**: balance between evidence capture and right to honor
- **Cross-border electronic evidence**: mutual recognition with minimum authenticity standards

## 6. Checklist for Litigating Lawyers

### Pre-litigation

- [ ] Assess whether audiovisual evidence will be challenged as deepfake
- [ ] Document chain of custody with hashes and timestamps
- [ ] Budget for computer expert evidence (€800-3,000 depending on complexity)
- [ ] Verify if C2PA standards exist in client's devices

### During Litigation

- [ ] Request authenticity expert evidence if proof is contested
- [ ] Require opponent to provide complete metadata and original file
- [ ] Investigate if alternative versions of the same recording exist
- [ ] Prepare corroborating witness testimony on content of the evidence

### Post-litigation

- [ ] Archive verification methodology for future similar cases
- [ ] Update internal firm protocols
- [ ] Train team in basic detection tools

Deepfakes are not a future threat: they are a procedural reality in 2026. Law firms that incorporate **technical verification protocols** and **chain of custody documentation** will have a competitive advantage both when presenting and challenging audiovisual evidence.

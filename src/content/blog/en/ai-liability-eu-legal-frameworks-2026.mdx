## Introduction

The **AI Liability Directive** represents a fundamental milestone in the European Union's artificial intelligence regulatory framework. Approved in 2025 with full implementation in 2026, this regulation establishes clear rules for determining liability when AI systems cause harm to natural persons. For Spanish law firms, understanding these new legal frameworks is not only a regulatory obligation but a strategic opportunity to advise clients deploying AI solutions.

## 1. The AI Liability Directive: Overview

### Context and Objectives

The AI Liability Directive complements the **AI Act** and the **European Cybersecurity Framework**, creating a complete regulatory ecosystem addressing three fundamental pillars:
- **Product regulation** (AI Act)
- **Cybersecurity** (CRA, NIS2, DORA)
- **Civil liability** (AI Liability Directive)

**Key objectives:**
- Harmonize civil liability regimes for damages caused by AI
- Reduce the burden of proof for claimants
- Facilitate access to evidence in litigation cases

### Scope of Application

The Directive applies to:
- **AI systems** of any risk level (including low-risk AI)
- **Natural persons** who suffer material or immaterial damage
- **Providers and deployers** of AI systems in the EU market

### Exclusions

Does not apply to:
- Criminal liability
- Family law
- Contract law (with exceptions)
- Damages caused by public administration

## 2. New Liability Regimes

### Strict Liability for Defective Products

**Scope:** AI systems classified as "product" under the Product Liability Directive

**Burden of proof requirements:**
```markdown
- [ ] Demonstrate that the AI system is a "product"
- [ ] Identify the defect in the system
- [ ] Prove the damage caused
- [ ] Establish causation between defect and damage
```

**AI defects:**
- Design defects (algorithm errors, biases)
- Manufacturing defects (incorrect implementation)
- Information defects (insufficient instructions)

### Duty of Care Non-Compliance Liability Regime

**Scope:** Situations where product liability does not apply

**New causal presumptions:**
1. **Causal evidence presumption:**
   - Presumption that duty of care breach caused the damage
   - Claimant must demonstrate the breach and damage
   - Defendant can rebut this presumption

2. **Defect presumption:**
   - When claimant cannot access necessary evidence
   - Applicable to high-risk AI systems (Annex III AI Act)

## 3. Evidence Disclosure: New Right of Access

### Evidence Disclosure Mechanism

**What includes:**
- Training and validation records
- Technical documentation
- System operation logs
- Algorithms and parameters used

### Request Process

```markdown
1. Formal request from claimant
2. Court evaluation of:
   - Necessity of evidence
   - Proportionality of measure
   - Protection of trade secrets
3. Court order to provider/deployer
4. Confidential information protection measures
5. Sanctions for improper information use
```

### Protections for Providers

**Confidentiality measures:**
- Redaction of sensitive information
- Restricted expert access
- Use of "clean rooms" for review
- Sanctions for misuse of information

## 5. Compliance Strategies for Clients

### Liability Risk Management Framework

**Phase 1: Identification**
- Map all deployed AI systems
- Classify by liability risk level
- Identify products vs. services

**Phase 2: Assessment**
```markdown
- [ ] Technical documentation audit
- [ ] Validation process review
- [ ] Defect risk analysis
- [ ] Insurance coverage evaluation
```

**Phase 3: Mitigation**
- Documentation and traceability improvements
- Implement explainability mechanisms
- Contract specialized insurance
- Establish evidence retention protocols

**Phase 4: Monitoring**
- Incident and claim KPIs
- Periodic compliance reviews
- Policy updates per jurisprudence

### Essential Documentation

**Records to maintain:**

| Document | Retention | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| System decision logs | 5 years | Traceability and causation |
| Training documentation | 5 years | Evidence of non-defect |
| Validations and tests | 5 years | Due care |
| Usage policies | 2 years + active | User instructions |
| Incidents and corrections | 5 years | System evolution |

### Contracts and Exemption Clauses

**Recommended clauses:**

1. **Liability exemption for misuse:**
   - Exclude liability for unauthorized use
   - Establish usage limits
   - Define force majeure cases

2. **Cross-indemnification:**
   - Protection against third-party claims
   - Indemnification limits
   - Joint defense mechanisms

3. **Security and maintenance:**
   - Update obligations
   - Incident reporting protocols
   - Cooperation in investigations

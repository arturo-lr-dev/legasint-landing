## Introduction

The **EU Artificial Intelligence Regulation (AI Act)** has entered into full force in 2026, transforming how law firms use AI tools. With the maturation of systems like GPT-5, Claude Opus, and specialized LegalTech solutions, the legal sector is at a critical juncture: **leverage AI or fall behind**.

However, using these technologies comes with specific obligations that many professionals still don't fully understand. This practical guide details what your firm should do **now** to comply with the AI Act.

## What is the AI Act and Why Does it Matter?

The AI Act is the **world's first comprehensive regulatory framework** on artificial intelligence. It establishes a risk-based classification system:

- **Unacceptable risk:** Prohibited (e.g., social scoring systems)
- **High risk:** Strict regulation (e.g., AI in judicial decisions)
- **Limited risk:** Transparency obligations
- **Minimal risk:** No special restrictions

For law firms, most AI tools fall into the **high-risk or limited-risk** categories, depending on their use.

## Classification of AI Systems in the Legal Sector

### High Risk

AI systems that directly influence **critical legal decisions**:

- **Predictive litigation analytics** that guide procedural strategies
- **Automated contract review systems** with binding recommendations
- **Automated due diligence** for M&A transactions
- **Risk assessment tools** in compliance

**Obligations:**
- Registration in the EU database
- Third-party conformity assessment
- Robust risk management system
- Continuous human oversight
- Extensive technical documentation
- Activity logs retained for minimum 6 months

### Limited Risk

**Assistance and productivity** tools that don't make final decisions:

- **AI chatbots** for initial client inquiries
- **Draft generators** for contracts or legal briefs
- **Legal research assistants** for case law
- **Automatic document summaries**

**Obligations:**
- **Transparency:** Clearly inform users they're interacting with AI
- **Explainability:** Ability to explain how the system works
- **Human oversight:** A lawyer must review and validate results

## Practical Steps for Compliance

### 1. AI Tools Audit (URGENT)

**Immediate action:**

```markdown
✅ Complete inventory of AI tools in use
✅ Classification by risk level according to AI Act
✅ Identification of systems without adequate human oversight
✅ Review of contracts with AI providers
```

**Key question for each tool:**
> "If this system fails or provides an incorrect answer, what is the impact on our clients and cases?"

### 2. Implement Human Oversight (Human-in-the-Loop)

The AI Act requires that **no high-risk system operates without effective human supervision**.

**Recommended protocol:**

1. **Input:** Senior lawyer reviews the prompt or request
2. **Process:** AI system processes with logs enabled
3. **Output:** Mandatory review of result before use
4. **Validation:** Digital signature from responsible lawyer

**Practical example:**

```python
# Contract review workflow with AI
def review_contract_ai(document):
    # 1. AI system analyzes
    ai_analysis = ai_system.analyze(document)
    
    # 2. Log to system (AI Act mandatory)
    log_system(timestamp, user, document_id, ai_analysis)
    
    # 3. MANDATORY human review
    lawyer_review = senior_lawyer.review(ai_analysis)
    
    # 4. Only if lawyer approves, use it
    if lawyer_review.approved:
        return ai_analysis
    else:
        return lawyer_review.manual_correction
```

### 3. Transparency with Clients

**Legal obligation:** Inform clients when AI is used in their matters.

**Recommended clause for service contracts:**

> "This firm uses artificial intelligence tools to optimize legal research, document analysis, and draft preparation. All AI-generated results are reviewed and validated by licensed attorneys before use. Clients may request at any time that their matter be handled exclusively by humans at no additional cost."

### 4. Documentation and Logs

**AI Act requirements for high-risk systems:**

- **Automatic logs:** Record of each system use
- **Minimum retention:** 6 months (recommended: 2 years)
- **Information to record:**
  - Date and time of use
  - Responsible user
  - System input and output
  - Final decision made (approved/modified/rejected)

**Recommended tools:**
- Document management systems with complete traceability
- Legal Project Management platforms with audit capabilities
- Specialized AI Governance solutions (e.g., Legartis, Kira Systems with compliance modules)

### 5. Team Training

**Mandatory:** All personnel using AI systems must receive specific training.

**Minimum training program:**

- **Module 1:** AI Act basics (2 hours)
- **Module 2:** Identifying risks in legal AI use (3 hours)
- **Module 3:** Human oversight protocols (2 hours)
- **Module 4:** AI incident and error management (2 hours)

**Certification:** Lawyers must accredit training annually.

## Practical Cases: How to Comply Without Giving Up AI

### Case 1: Using ChatGPT for Drafts

**Situation:** A junior associate uses ChatGPT-5 to generate draft briefs.

**Compliance:**

✅ **Transparency:** Include disclaimer in document: "Draft generated with AI assistance, reviewed by [lawyer name]"

✅ **Oversight:** Complete review of draft by senior lawyer before filing

✅ **Logs:** Internal record of use (date, case, prompt used)

✅ **Training:** Junior must complete training on limitations of generative AI

### Case 2: Predictive Litigation Success System

**Situation:** The firm implements a system that predicts success probabilities in litigation based on case law.

**Classification:** **High risk** (influences critical decisions)

**Mandatory compliance:**

✅ **EU Registration:** System must be registered in the European high-risk AI systems database

✅ **Conformity assessment:** Audit by notified body

✅ **Technical documentation:** Detailed explanation of algorithm, training data, and margin of error

✅ **Continuous oversight:** Monthly monitoring of system accuracy

✅ **Right to explanation:** Ability to explain each prediction to the client

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

The AI Act establishes **significant** fines for non-compliance:

- **Up to €35 million** or 7% of annual global turnover (whichever is higher) for violations of prohibitions
- **Up to €15 million** or 3% of turnover for failure to comply with high-risk system obligations
- **Up to €7.5 million** or 1.5% for inaccurate information or transparency violations

**Additionally:** Possible **civil liability** to clients if improper use of AI causes damages.

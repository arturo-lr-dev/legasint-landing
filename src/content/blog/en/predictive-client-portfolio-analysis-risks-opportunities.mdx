## Introduction

Did you know that 70% of clients who leave a law firm never explicitly announce it? They simply stop contacting you, delay payments, or gradually reduce the number of matters they entrust. By the time the partner detects it, it's already too late.

**Predictive portfolio analysis** is changing this dynamic. Instead of reacting when a client leaves, the most advanced firms use their own data to **anticipate behaviors**, identify who is at risk of leaving, and discover hidden growth opportunities within their existing client base.

In this article, we explore how to implement a predictive analysis system in your firm—without needing to be a multinational or having a data science team.

## What is predictive portfolio analysis?

Predictive analysis applies statistical models and machine learning algorithms to historical data to **predict future behaviors**. In the context of a law firm, this translates into:

- **Churn prediction**: Which clients have a high probability of not renewing or stopping sending matters?
- **Upsell identification**: Which current clients might need additional services they're not currently contracting?
- **Portfolio risk assessment**: Are you concentrating too much billing on a few clients? What happens if you lose the main one?
- **Price optimization**: Which clients are willing to pay more for higher-value services?

### Key difference: reactive vs. proactive

| Reactive approach | Predictive approach |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Client doesn't renew, you react | You detect warning signals 3-6 months in advance |
| You lose revenue and don't know why | You identify causes and act preventively |
| You discover opportunities by chance | You prioritize expansions with highest probability of success |
| You make decisions based on intuition | You make decisions based on data |

## Data you already have and aren't using

The good news: law firms generate **more data than they think**. You don't need to install sensors or buy external datasets. The data is already in your systems:

### 1. Billing and payment data
- Amount billed per client and month
- Billing frequency
- Average payment days
- Variations in billed amount (upward or downward trends)
- Non-payments or recurring delays

### 2. Activity data
- Number of open matters per client
- Matter typology (is complexity decreasing?)
- Time between consecutive matters
- Contact channels (did the client stop calling and now only sends brief emails?)

### 3. Relationship data
- Client tenure
- Number of contacts within the client (do you only speak to one person?)
- Participation in events or newsletters
- Responses to satisfaction surveys
- Referrals generated by that client

### 4. Market data
- Client's sector (is it in decline?)
- Corporate news (mergers, layoffs, management changes)
- Client lifecycle (growing startup vs. established company)

## Warning signals that predict churn

Predictive analysis identifies patterns that, analyzed individually, seem harmless but together are highly predictive:

### Early signals (3-6 months before)
- **Gradual billing reduction**: -15% for two consecutive quarters
- **Increase in payment days**: from 30 to 60 days on average
- **Lower matter diversity**: the client only sends one type of work, before it was 3-4
- **Change of contact person**: the person you had a relationship with left the company
- **Lower contact frequency**: from monthly calls to sporadic emails

### Critical signals (1-3 months before)
- **Request for complete file copy**: classic indicator they're evaluating another firm
- **Questioning of rates**: aggressive negotiations that didn't happen before
- **Absence from strategic meetings**: they stop inviting you to planning meetings
- **Hiring of in-house counsel**: they're building internal capacity

## How to build your predictive model (without being a data expert)

You don't need to hire a data scientist to get started. There are three implementation levels depending on your firm's maturity:

### Level 1: Manual analysis with Excel (immediate start)

**Duration**: 1-2 days of initial work, 30 minutes weekly maintenance.

**Steps**:
1. Export the last 24 months of billing per client
2. Calculate for each client:
   - Total billing per quarter
   - Quarterly variation (%)
   - Average payment days
   - Number of open matters
3. Create a "risk score" column with simple rules:
   - +1 point if billing dropped >10% this quarter
   - +1 point if payment days increased >15 days
   - +1 point if number of matters dropped
   - +1 point if no new matters in 90 days
4. Clients with 3+ points: **priority review** this week

**Tools**: Excel, Google Sheets, or Notion with formulas.

### Level 2: Dashboard with no-code tools (1-2 weeks)

**Duration**: 1-2 weeks initial setup, automated afterwards.

**Recommended stack**:
- **Data source**: Monthly export from your management software (Prymus, Wolters Kluwer, or ERP)
- **Processing**: [n8n](https://n8n.io) or [Make](https://make.com) to automate extraction and transformation
- **Visualization**: [Google Looker Studio](https://lookerstudio.google.com) (free) or [Power BI](https://powerbi.microsoft.com)
- **Alerts**: Automatic notifications by email or Slack when a client changes risk category

**Recommended dashboards**:
1. **Portfolio heat map**: billing vs. tenure, colored by risk
2. **Billing trends**: line chart by top 20 clients
3. **Churn alerts**: list of clients with high risk score
4. **Upsell opportunities**: clients with billing growth but low service diversification

### Level 3: Predictive model with AI (1-3 months)

For firms with >50 lawyers or annual billing >€10M.

**Options**:
- **Hire a specialized service**: companies like [Gavelytics](https://gavelytics.com) (litigation-focused) or data consultancies applied to legal
- **Develop internally**: using Python + scikit-learn, with a part-time data analyst
- **Use predictive BI platforms**: tools like [DataRobot](https://datarobot.com) or [H2O.ai](https://h2o.ai) that automate model creation

**Advanced model variables**:
- Billing history (12-24 months)
- Temporal sequence of matters
- Communication metadata (frequency, channel, tone)
- External data (sector, company size, news)
- Marketing interactions (newsletter opens, events)

**Model success metrics**:
- **Precision**: What % of clients marked as "at risk" actually leave? (target: >60%)
- **Recall**: What % of clients who leave were detected in advance? (target: >70%)
- **Lead time**: How many months in advance do we detect the risk? (target: >3 months)

## Practical cases: what you'll discover

### Case 1: The client who seemed healthy

A Madrid firm analyzed its portfolio and discovered that a real estate sector client—billing €180k annually—had reduced matter frequency by 40% in 6 months. Total billing was maintained because remaining matters were more complex (and expensive), but volume was dropping.

**Action**: The responsible partner scheduled a strategic meeting. They discovered the client had hired an in-house lawyer for routine matters. They reoriented the relationship toward complex matters and higher-value transactions, recovering billing and strengthening the relationship.

### Case 2: The hidden upsell opportunity

A commercial law firm detected that 3 technology sector clients had grown 200% in their own billing in 12 months, but the firm's billing only grew 5%. These clients were contracting legal services from other firms for new needs (data protection, international contracting) that the original firm could cover.

**Action**: Targeted "account development" campaign: needs discovery sessions, integrated service proposals. Result: +€340k additional annual billing in 6 months.

### Case 3: Risk concentration

A portfolio analysis revealed that 45% of annual billing came from a single client. The simulation model showed that if that client left, the firm would have to lay off 30% of the team.

**Action**: Active diversification plan: objective to reduce dependence on the top client to 25% in 18 months through client acquisition in similar but uncorrelated sectors.

## How to act on predictions

Detecting risk is only the first step. The important thing is to **act**:

### For clients at risk of churn

| Lead time | Recommended action | Responsible |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| 6 months | Strategic value meeting: how do we help achieve your objectives? | Managing partner |
| 3 months | Proposal for additional service or rate review | Partner + Business development |
| 1 month | Specific retention offer, possible adjustment of conditions | Managing partner |
| Already left | Exit interview + lessons learned analysis | Management |

### For upsell opportunities

1. **Gap analysis**: What services does the client need that you're not offering?
2. **Decision map**: Who makes the purchasing decision for those services? Is it the same person?
3. **Value proposition**: Don't sell "more services," sell "solution to a problem they have"
4. **Pilot test**: Offer a first matter at reduced rate to demonstrate value

## Challenges and how to overcome them

### "We don't have enough data"

**Reality**: With 2 years of billing and 20 active clients, you already have enough data for basic analysis. You don't need Big Data, you need **good questions**.

### "Our data is messy"

**Solution**: Start with what you have. Even imperfect data is better than intuition. Use the 80/20: 20% of well-structured data will give you 80% of the value.

### "Lawyers don't trust algorithms"

**Strategy**: Don't replace intuition, amplify it. The model suggests; the lawyer decides. Start with cases where the model is right and build trust gradually.

### "We don't have time for this"

**Reality**: Investing 4 hours weekly analyzing your portfolio can prevent losing a client representing €150k annually. The ROI is immediate.

## Conclusion

Predictive portfolio analysis is not a technological fad: it's a **core competency** for firms that want to grow sustainably. In an increasingly competitive market, knowing who is leaving before they leave, and who could buy more before they do so with someone else, is a decisive advantage.

**You don't need to be Amazon to use analytics**. Start with an Excel sheet, identify your 5 most at-risk clients this week, and schedule a meeting with each one. That first step will put you ahead of 80% of firms.
